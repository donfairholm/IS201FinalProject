2025 Europe Trip

Vienna, Austria

August 7-10

Vienna is the capital of Austria and a city steeped in imperial history. Once the center of the powerful Habsburg Empire, it’s known for its opulent palaces like Schönbrunn and the Hofburg, elegant coffee houses, and rich musical heritage — having been home to Mozart, Beethoven, and Strauss. Vienna blends classical charm with modern sophistication, offering grand architecture, world-class museums, and a vibrant cultural scene. Today, it's consistently ranked as one of the world's most livable cities.

Bad Gams, Austria

August 11

Bad Gams is a small spa town in the Styria region of Austria, known for its natural thermal springs and tranquil countryside. Nestled among rolling hills and vineyards, it’s a charming destination for relaxation and wellness tourism. Bad Gams offers a peaceful retreat with scenic hiking trails, traditional Styrian hospitality, and opportunities to sample local wines and regional cuisine. It's a lesser-known gem perfect for experiencing authentic Austrian rural life.

Budapest, Hungry

August 12-14

Budapest, Hungary’s capital, is famously called the "Pearl of the Danube." The city is split by the Danube River into Buda and Pest, each with its own character — Buda with its historic castle and hills, and Pest with its lively streets and grand boulevards. Known for its dramatic history, thermal baths, and stunning architecture (like the Parliament building and Fisherman’s Bastion), Budapest offers a blend of old-world charm and modern vibrancy. It’s also famous for its ruin bars and rich culinary scene.

Munich, Germany

August 15-18

Munich is the capital of Bavaria and one of Germany's most iconic cities. Known worldwide for Oktoberfest, its festive beer culture, and traditional Bavarian heritage, Munich also boasts elegant architecture, including the Neues Rathaus and Nymphenburg Palace. The city is a major cultural and economic hub, home to prestigious museums, car giants like BMW, and beautiful parks like the English Garden. With the Alps nearby, it’s a gateway for both urban exploration and outdoor adventure.
